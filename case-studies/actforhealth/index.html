Website for the Ukraine-Swiss Project "Act for Health"

Situation: The non-governmental organization aimed to enhance its online presence and establish a convenient communication platform. The organization also sought to report on the work of their social campaigns and consolidate all created content in a unified environment. To achieve these goals, it was decided to develop a new website tailored to the organization's needs.
Challenges:
  1. Design Alignment: Creating a design that reflects the organization's values and mission.
  2. Functional Development: Designing and implementing functional features.
  3. Responsive Design: Ensuring optimal website viewing across various devices, including mobile phones and tablets.
Strategy:
  1. Research: Understanding the organization's mission, values, and requirements to develop a fitting website.
  2. Design and UX: Crafting a design that conveys the organization's identity and provides a positive user experience.
  3. Development: Developing and incorporating functional features on the website, including interactive forms, blogs, galleries, and more.
  4. Adaptation: Implementing responsive design to ensure optimal display on different devices.
Execution:
  1. Design and Development: Creating the design and functional aspects based on the strategy's earlier stages.
  2. Testing: Verifying functionality, adaptability, and proper display across various browsers and devices.
  3. Implementation: Hosting the website and configuring the domain.

Results:

Enhanced Online Presence

The new website allowed the organization to showcase its work and values to a wider audience.

Increased Interaction:

Improved communication with organization members and supporters through interactive site elements.

User Convenience

Easy access to information.

This case study example illustrates how website development can help achieve the goals of a non-governmental organization and amplify its impact through the Internet.
